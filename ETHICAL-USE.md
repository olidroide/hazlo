# Ethical Use Policy

This document defines the hazlo project's ethical use policy.

hazlo is source-available software. It is not open source in the OSI sense.

## Prohibited Uses

The software must not be used:

1. For military, weapons, or defense operations.
2. For intelligence, targeting, or operational support in armed conflict.
3. For mass surveillance of civilian populations.
4. For systems intended to repress freedom of expression, association, assembly,
   due process, or other internationally recognized human rights.
5. For projects that facilitate war crimes, crimes against humanity, genocide,
   ethnic cleansing, forced displacement, or similar serious human rights abuses.

## Clarifications

- Personal, educational, research, and internal business use are permitted.
- Private individuals and private companies may use the software for permitted
  purposes.
- NGOs, humanitarian organizations, and human rights defenders are permitted to
  use the software for lawful and non-harmful purposes.
- Contributions, patches, forks, and improvement proposals are welcome, provided
  they do not support prohibited uses.

## Questions

If you are unsure whether your use case is compatible with this policy, contact
the licensor before use.

## Important Note

This project is source-available and policy-restricted. It should not be
described as "open source" in the OSI sense, because these restrictions limit
certain categories of use.
